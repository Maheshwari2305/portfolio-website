Ecommerce Sales & Profitability Dashboard

An end-to-end business intelligence solution transforming unorganized, complex transactional records into clean, actionable executive dashboards.

01 Discovery & Planning

Project Overview

  • Interactive Dashboard Solution: Designs and develops a comprehensive Retail Sales Analysis Dashboard using Microsoft Power BI to replace traditional static spreadsheet reports.
  • Transforms Raw Data: Turns unutilized daily transactional records (sales, customer purchases, and product inventory) into clear, meaningful visual insights.
  • Improves Reporting Efficiency: Delivers automated calculations, real-time chart interaction, KPI tracking, and executive-level summaries for fast decision-making.

Business Understanding

  • Addresses Retail Challenges: Identifies the business need to track high-performing items, regional profit margins, and seasonal sales trends.
  • Evaluates Store Obstacles: Focuses on answering how different customer segments purchase products and how discount strategies impact overall net profit.
  • Applies BI Lifecycle: Handles the complete Business Intelligence lifecycle including data preparation, exploratory data analysis, data modeling, and business reporting.

Business Requirements & Objectives

  • Monitors Financial Performance: Tracks core business metrics including historical revenue patterns, net profit, quantities sold, and discount behaviors across multiple years.
  • Isolates Performance Drivers: Sets up tools to easily identify high-performing and underperforming product categories across different geographic regions.
  • Analyzes Customer Behavior: Groups and evaluates purchasing behaviors across various corporate and customer segments.
  • Generates Strategic Takeaways: Develops a clear presentation framework for executive-level recommendations to improve the store's future business performance.

02 Data Collection

Data Sourcing Strategy

  • Base Retail Dataset: Sources an enterprise-grade retail transactional dataset from Kaggle containing 9,000+ clean records.
  • Synthetic Anomaly Injection: Uses Generative AI to intentionally inject realistic human entry errors, structural messiness, and logical bugs into the dataset.
  • Scaled Environment: Expands the dataset footprint into a robust testing environment of 20,000+ total records to test automated data auditing at scale.
  • Professional Experience Simulation: Design-engineers specific database challenges to systematically test data quality frameworks in both Excel and Power Query.

Dataset Dimension Fields

  • Monitors Transaction Identifiers: Evaluates tracking columns including unique Order IDs, Customer Names, and localized City or Region parameters.
  • Tracks Financial Variables: Structures data properties capturing financial variables such as itemized Product Categories, Sales Revenue, Quantities, and Net Profit margins.
  • Captures Shipping Elements: Includes multi-year performance timelines detailing specific Order Dates, Shipment Dates, and localized shipping modes.

03 Data Cleaning

CLEAN Framework Implementation

  • Conceptualize the Data (C): Checked the key metrics, columns, and dimensions to understand what each row represented before changing any data.
  • Locate Solvable Issues (L): Eyeballed the data and filtered distinct values to build an issue log tracking inconsistent dates, bad categories, and duplicates.
  • Evaluate Unsolvable Issues (E): Separated and flagged systemic data quality bugs that had no source of truth, like missing order dates or negative shipping durations.
  • Augment the Data (A): Improved the dataset by splitting customer names into components and creating helper columns to track business logic breaks.
  • Note and Document (N): Created a detailed table log tracking the column name, issue description, row counts, and your exact technical resolution.

Data Cleaning Strategy

  • Adopted Agile Principles: Focused on delivering data that is "good enough to analyze, share, and iterate on" instead of waiting for a perfect dataset.
  • Executed Three-Step Processing: Iterated through the data by first removing obvious dirty columns, standardizing text layouts, and finally refining values after initial analysis.

Excel Data Cleaning

  • Fixed Inconsistent Categorizations: Used TRIM, PROPER, and XLOOKUP functions to populate 163 missing product category values based on valid product details.
  • Standardized Text Data: Cleared out blank, whitespace-only, and hyphen ("-") entries inside the Region and City columns, standardizing invalid values to "Missing".
  • Enforced String Formatting: Corrected structural formatting for the Postal Code column by using the TEXT function to restore missing leading zeros and enforce a standard 5-digit layout.
  • Manually Populated Records: Investigated missing postal codes for Burlington, Vermont, and manually populated them with the correct verified ZIP code (05401).

Power Query Staging Transformations

  • Removed Structural Waste: Dropped a completely Unnamed Column affecting 66 rows that held zero business value.
  • Standardized System Timestamps: Uniformed all records in both the Order Date and Shipment Date columns into a clean, matching DD-MMM-YY date format structure.
  • Parsed Customer Attributes: Split full names into separate component columns while preserving the original column to keep reporting features flexible.

Handling Logical Exceptions & Anomalies

  • Retained Systemic Nulls: Kept 327 missing order dates as null values and flagged them as quality bugs because the source data could not be reliably inferred.
  • Tracked Business Logic Violations: Identified 55 rows where the shipment date occurred before the order date; created a helper column to calculate shipment days and flagged the negative anomalies for review.
  • Flagged Zero Quantities: Isolated 39 rows where the item quantity equaled zero despite having a positive sales value, highlighting them as a business rule exception.
  • Isolated Financial Anomalies: Retained and flagged 41 negative sales rows (representing potential returns or adjustments) and 168 profit anomalies exceeding 200% of sales values for separate business validation.

04 Data Modeling

SCAN Framework Execution

  • Mapped Stakeholder Goals (S): Identified exactly which KPIs and dimensions mattered most to the business, aligning the dashboard view with core themes like revenue performance, regional margins, and discount impacts.
  • Scoped Core Data (C): Organized the raw dataset schema into clean, logical groups covering customer data, product categories, order variables, and geography fields.
  • Analyzed Aggregates & Anomalies (A): Evaluated high-level metrics and outlier patterns to establish target outputs for automated trend detection and anomaly tracking.
  • Targeted Notable Segments (N): Sliced the transactional records by specific timeframes and categories to surface early business insights during the modeling phase.

SQL Analysis & MySQL Ingestion

  • Prepared Bulk Staging: Saved the cleaned retail data as a CSV UTF-8 file from Excel and moved it to the secure MySQL server upload directory.
  • Authenticated Server Pipeline: Opened the Windows Command Prompt, navigated directly to the MySQL bin directory, and connected to the server as root to access the command-line client.
  • Executed High-Speed Ingestion: Selected the project database using USE ecommerce_project;, truncated the old tables to clean up previous data, and ran the LOAD DATA INFILE command to rapidly import the entire dataset.
  • Validated Structural Integrity: Verified the row counts using SELECT COUNT(*) to ensure all records loaded successfully, and ran LIMIT 10 queries to confirm precise data integrity and DATE datatype conversions.

Relational Normalization Strategy

  • Identifies Redundancy Issues: Spotted structural data duplication where customer descriptions and product details repeated inefficiently on every individual transaction row.
  • Normalized Flat Table Architecture: Separated the single, messy raw table by breaking down the fields and creating distinct, specialized entity tables.
  • Improved Database Maintainability: Created an organized system where each unique customer and product record was stored only once, drastically reducing redundancy and improving scalability.

Star Schema Architecture

  • Defined Dimension Layer: Created Customer_Dim (storing names and segments) and Product_Dim (storing sub-categories) using Customer_ID and Product_ID as primary keys.
  • Established Fact Layer: Structured the central Order_Fact table using Fact_ID as the primary key to hold numeric transactional metrics like sales, quantities, discounts, and profits.
  • Configured Relationships: Connected the tables together using clear One-to-Many relationships, linking the dimension lookup keys straight into the central fact table.
  • Optimized Query Performance: Achieved faster query execution times, cleaner aggregations, and an industry-standard dashboard schema perfect for reporting tools.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

  • Analyzed Financial Health Metrics: Calculated baseline aggregations across the entire dataset to establish global benchmarks for Total Sales, Net Profit, and overall Profit Margin percentages.
  • Investigated the Discount Impact: Ran distribution checks on discount percentages to isolate how promotional pricing scales against net profitability, identifying severe margin drops past specific discount thresholds.
  • Profiled Product Category Performance: Sliced transactional volumes against specific categories and sub-categories to instantly separate consistent high-margin items from high-revenue loss-leaders.
  • Evaluated Customer Purchasing Patterns: Grouped order records by corporate and consumer segments to evaluate average order values, quantities per transaction, and return frequencies.
  • Mapped Regional Demand Dynamics: Aggregated sales performance across geographic regions and cities to pinpoint underperforming territories and identify high-demand localized markets.
  • Tracked Time-Series Seasonal Trends: Audited historical sales performance across a multi-year timeline to capture critical monthly and quarterly seasonal spikes.

05 Dashboard Design

DASH Framework Execution

  • Managed Duplicates & Visual Noise (D): Cleared out redundant charts, excessive gridlines, and overlapping labels to keep the reporting space clean and focused.
  • Isolated Performance Anomalies (A): Built clear visual alerts and conditional formatting rules to automatically highlight critical profit drops and high-discount exceptions.
  • Enforced Standardization (S): Unified all font hierarchies, currency definitions, and button placements across every page to provide a seamless user experience.
  • Handled Blank & Null Views (H): Designed defensive data layouts to ensure that empty fields or unpopulated region filters do not break chart elements during live cross-filtering.

Dashboard Planning & Visual Strategy

  • Structured a Multi-Page Interface: Designed a logical 3-page user path consisting of a Sales Dashboard, Product Dashboard, and an Executive Summary.
  • Integrated Interactive Controls: Implemented dynamic slicers for regions, order years, and product categories to allow users to customize their view on the fly.
  • Established Clear Navigation: Configured user-friendly page-navigation buttons and interactive bookmarks to let stakeholders move smoothly between deep-dives.

Visual Selection Matrix

  • Applied KPI Status Cards: Placed bold summary boxes at the very top of the screens to display immediate numbers for Total Sales, Profit, and Orders.
  • Selected Line Charts: Utilized continuous line visualizations to track monthly and quarterly sales growth trends over the multi-year timeline.
  • Deployed Donut Visuals: Used compact donut charts to show clean market share distributions across main customer segments and high-level product categories.
  • Leveraged Horizontal Bar Charts: Chosen to rank localized sub-category performance and regional revenues cleanly without text wrapping issues.

Interface Breakdowns

  • Sales Performance Page: Focused on global revenue trends, localized geographic mapping, and order quantity distributions.
  • Product Profitability Page: Created a dedicated area to drill down into product categories to isolate high-margin lines from loss-leaders.
  • Executive Summary Layer: Built a high-level manager space that translates complex visual charts directly into clear operational action items.

06 Insights & Recommendations

Business Insights

  • Analyzed Seasonal Sales Trends: Discovered that sales growth peaks sharply in March, while October and November show consistent declines, revealing a clear seasonal demand pattern.
  • Evaluated Customer Segments: Identified that the Consumer segment contributes 51.68% of total sales, significantly outperforming the Corporate (29.43%) and Home Office (18.89%) groups.
  • Isolated Low-Margin Categories: Found that the Furniture category records the weakest profitability despite maintaining reasonable sales volume, pointing to inefficient pricing or discount strategies.
  • Mapped Regional Revenues: Measured that the West region generates the highest sales ($1.39M), while the South region contributes the lowest ($0.94M), highlighting regional growth opportunities.
  • Ranked Sub-Category Performance: Ranked transactional items to show that Phones and Accessories dominate sales, while Fasteners remains the weakest-performing sub-category.
  • Uncovered the Sales vs. Profit Trap: Proved that high sales do not always mean high profits; highlighted that Cisco TelePresence generates strong sales but only a 0.82% profit margin, while HON Chairs operate at a -4.94% profit margin.
  • Audited Discounting Practices: Confirmed that Furniture receives one of the highest average discounts (16.09%) while generating the lowest profitability, showing that current discounting practices reduce margins without delivering a proportional return.

Strategic Recommendations

  • Implement Seasonal Planning: Align promotions, inventory planning, and staffing with the March demand peak, while introducing targeted campaigns during October–November to reduce seasonal slowdowns.
  • Revamp Pricing Strategy: Re-evaluate Furniture pricing and discount policies by testing lower discount levels or premium bundle offers to improve profitability.
  • Optimize Category Performance: Develop focused marketing strategies for underperforming categories such as Furniture and Fasteners to improve their sales contribution.
  • Review Low-Margin Products: Review high-sales, low-margin products (like Cisco TelePresence and HON Chairs) to identify pricing, procurement, or cost optimization opportunities.
  • Target Regional Growth: Investigate the factors contributing to the South region's lower performance and replicate successful practices from the high-performing West region.
  • Expand Customer Segments: Continue leveraging the strong Consumer segment while expanding targeted campaigns for Corporate and Home Office customers to diversify revenue streams.
  • Capture Growth Opportunities: Continue investing in high-performing categories such as Technology and Office Supplies, while maintaining inventory and promotional support for top-performing products like the Canon ImageClass series.

Challenges & Lessons Learned

  • Faced Complex Data Anomaly Auditing: Managed the tracking of synthetic human entry errors and invalid business logic rows across a large dataset without breaking report structures.
  • Balanced Volume vs. Value Visuals: Solved the challenge of representing high-volume, negative-margin items on the same chart layout without hiding baseline patterns.
  • Learned Scalable Schema Design: Confirmed that investing time up front in proper SQL database normalization makes dashboard construction significantly cleaner.

Future Improvements

  • Integrate Machine Learning Forecasting: Plan to incorporate predictive modeling to forecast future monthly demand and optimize inventory levels before seasonal peaks.
  • Add Granular Profit Drill-Throughs: Aim to build automated drill-through summary sub-pages to trace individual transaction returns back to original promotional codes.
